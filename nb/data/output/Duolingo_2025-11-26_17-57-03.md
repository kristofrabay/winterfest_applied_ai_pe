Duolingo (DUOL) – Research Memo  
As of November 26, 2025  

TOPIC 1 – COMPETITION / MARKET POSITION  

Industry structure and market  
- Duolingo describes the online language learning industry as “highly competitive, with a consistent stream of new products and entrants” and low switching costs.  
- Market options for learners include:  
  - Online/app-based language platforms  
  - Offline language schools, tutoring, textbooks and classroom-based programs  
  - Other learning domains overlapping Duolingo’s platform: language assessment providers, literacy platforms, and math/music learning products.  

Competitive set (as defined by the company)  
- Duolingo states it competes for “time, attention, and share of wallet” with:  
  - Other online and app-based language learning platforms  
  - Offline forms of language learning  
  - Language learning assessment providers (for Duolingo English Test)  
  - Literacy platforms and, increasingly, other kinds of online learning (math, music, etc.).  
- Potential competitors also include:  
  - Large technology and social media companies that can leverage existing user bases and data  
  - Mobile operating system and app store operators (e.g., platform owners that could launch competing products)  
  - Free language-learning products from private companies, universities, and government agencies; Duolingo estimates “thousands of free mobile applications for language learning,” across at least 50 languages.  

Competitive advantages Duolingo cites  
- Scale and brand:  
  - Flagship app described as the “world’s most popular way to learn languages” and “top-grossing Education app in the app stores.”  
  - As of Dec 31, 2024, Duolingo offered courses in 40+ languages to “more than 100 million monthly active users”; in the U.S., more people learn a language on Duolingo than in K–12 foreign language classes.  
  - The brand has strong pop-culture presence; word-of-mouth and social media are key acquisition drivers, enabling growth with relatively limited paid user acquisition.  
- Data and AI “moat”:  
  - Over 1 billion exercises completed every day on the platform, creating what the company believes is “the world’s largest learning dataset.”  
  - Heavy use of A/B testing (hundreds of tests per quarter) and machine learning to optimize engagement, difficulty, and learning outcomes.  
- Freemium model and virality:  
  - All core language-learning content is free; paid tiers add features but not core access, which expands the user funnel.  
  - Growth is driven by two “flywheels”:  
    - Learning flywheel: more learners → more data → better engagement and efficacy → more word-of‑mouth → more learners.  
    - Investment flywheel: large free user base and organic growth allow capital to be focused on product and data, not heavy paid marketing.  

Competitive challenges and risks (per filings)  
- Low switching costs and many free alternatives (podcasts, videos, apps, digital textbooks, etc.).  
- Competitors with hybrid offerings (apps plus physical classrooms/textbooks) may better meet certain learner preferences.  
- Larger companies may:  
  - Offer lower prices or free products focused on user acquisition rather than profitability  
  - Leverage existing platforms and user data to gain scale quickly  
  - Deploy emerging technologies such as generative AI to disrupt incumbents.  
- Free online translation services could reduce general interest in language learning, affecting demand.  

TOPIC 2 – CUSTOMERS AND REVENUE MODEL  

Customer base and segments  
- Mass-market consumer focus:  
  - Learners across the socioeconomic spectrum, “from billionaires and celebrities to recently resettled refugees.”  
  - Global user base; 100M+ MAUs as of Dec 31, 2024.  
- Use cases:  
  - Casual language learners, travelers, heritage speakers, and those needing English for economic opportunity.  
  - High-stakes test takers via Duolingo English Test (DET), used for university admissions and other English proficiency needs. DET accepted by >3,800 higher-education programs globally as of Dec 31, 2022, including top U.S. universities.  
  - Teachers and schools via Duolingo for Schools, a free web-based tool that lets teachers assign content and track progress.  

User metrics and engagement  
- Historical MAUs:  
  - ~60M MAUs (Dec 31, 2022).  
  - >85M MAUs (Dec 31, 2023).  
  - >100M MAUs (Dec 31, 2024).  
- DAUs and engagement ratios:  
  - 16.3M DAUs for the quarter ended Dec 31, 2022; DAU/MAU ratio 26.9% vs 23.8% a year earlier.  
  - Q2 2025: 128.3M MAUs (+24% YoY), 47.7M DAUs (+40% YoY).  
- Paid subscribers:  
  - As of Dec 31, 2022, paid subscribers were ~8% of MAUs.  
  - As of Dec 31, 2023, ~8% of MAUs were paid subscribers.  
  - As of Dec 31, 2024, ~9% of MAUs were paid subscribers.  
  - Q1 2025: 10.3M paid subscribers at period end (+40% YoY).  
  - Q2 2025: 10.9M paid subscribers (+37% YoY).  

Business model and revenue sources  
- Freemium language learning app:  
  - All course content is free to access indefinitely; monetization via:  
    - Premium subscription (“Super Duolingo” and higher tier “Duolingo Max”)  
    - Advertising shown to free users  
    - In-app purchases (IAPs) such as “Streak Freezes” and “Timer Boosts”  
    - Duolingo English Test fees  
    - Small, non-core businesses (e.g., “Duo’s Taquería” restaurant).  
- Subscription products:  
  - Super Duolingo: ad-free experience plus learning/gamification enhancements, including unlimited “Hearts.”  
  - Duolingo Max: launched 2023; a higher-priced tier including all Super features plus incremental generative-AI-powered features.  

Pricing and economics  
- A significant portion of revenue is subscription-based:  
  - FY 2024: $607.5M subscription revenue (+50% YoY), $140.5M “Other” revenue (+11% YoY; primarily ads, DET, and IAP), total revenue $748.0M (+41% YoY).  
  - In Q1 2025, subscriptions comprised 83% of revenue ($191.0M of $230.7M).  
- Subscription ARPU:  
  - In Q2 2025, subscription revenue per average paid subscriber grew ~6% YoY, driven by mix shift toward higher-priced tiers.  

Customer concentration and retention  
- The business is primarily direct-to-consumer with a very broad individual user base; filings do not highlight material customer concentration risk in any single institution or counterparty.  
- Engagement and retention are driven by gamification (streaks, leaderboards, points) and by continuously optimized, bite-sized lessons.  

TOPIC 3 – FINANCIALS  

Revenue and growth profile  
- Annual revenue (GAAP):  
  - 2021: $250.8M  
  - 2022: $369.5M  
  - 2023: $531.1M  
  - 2024: $748.0M (+41% YoY).  
- Revenue mix (2024):  
  - Subscriptions: $607.5M (81% of revenue), +50% YoY  
  - Other (ads, DET, IAPs, minor items): $140.5M (19% of revenue), +11% YoY.  
- 2025 run-rate and guidance:  
  - Q1 2025: revenue $230.7M (+38% YoY).  
  - Q2 2025: revenue $252.3M (+41% YoY).  
  - FY 2025 guidance: revenue $1,011–1,019M (+35–36% YoY); bookings $1,149–1,157M (+32–33% YoY).  

Profitability  
- Gross profit and margin:  
  - 2023: gross profit $389.0M on revenue $531.1M → gross margin ~73.2%.  
  - 2024: gross profit $544.4M on revenue $748.0M → gross margin ~72.8%, a 40 bps decline, mainly from lower advertising gross margin and higher AI-related costs; partially offset by a higher subscription mix.  
- Operating expenses (2024):  
  - R&D: $235.3M (+21% YoY); largest operating expense, driven mainly by headcount and stock-based compensation.  
  - Sales & marketing: $90.5M (+19% YoY), mainly increased direct marketing and personnel costs.  
  - G&A: $155.99M (from 132.1M in 2023), per income statement tool.  
- Net income:  
  - 2021: –$60.1M  
  - 2022: –$59.6M  
  - 2023: +$16.1M  
  - 2024: +$88.6M.  
- EBITDA and margins:  
  - 2023: EBITDA –$6.2M (normalized), EBIT –$13.3M.  
  - 2024: EBITDA $73.4M, EBIT $62.6M.  
  - Q1 2025: Adjusted EBITDA $62.8M (27.2% margin).  
  - Q2 2025: Adjusted EBITDA $78.7M (31.2% margin).  
  - FY 2025 guidance: Adjusted EBITDA $288.1–295.5M; margin 28.5–29.0%.  

Cash flow and capital intensity  
- Free cash flow (FCF):  
  - 2021: ~$3.0M FCF.  
  - 2022: $43.5M FCF.  
  - 2023: $139.9M FCF, with operating cash flow $153.6M and capex roughly $13.7M.  
  - 2024: $264.4M FCF, operating cash flow $285.5M, capex reported ~$9.0M (capital expenditures in cash flow statement –$21.1M, with some reclassifications).  
  - Q1 2025: FCF $103.0M on operating cash flow $105.6M.  
  - Q2 2025: FCF $86.3M on operating cash flow $90.7M.  
- Cash usage:  
  - Primary outflows are investments in securities, product development (R&D), limited capital expenditure, and lease obligations; shareholder capital returns are minimal (no notable buybacks in data provided).  

Balance sheet and liquidity (Dec 31, 2024)  
- Cash and equivalents:  
  - Cash and cash equivalents: $785.8M; cash + short-term investments: $877.6M.  
- Total assets: $2,403.3M.  
- Liabilities and equity:  
  - Total liabilities: $1,578.8M  
  - Stockholders’ equity: $824.6M (tangible book value ~$794.1M).  
- Debt-like items:  
  - Total “debt” on the balance sheet is capital lease obligations of ~$54.7M (long-term and current combined); there is no traditional financial debt disclosed in the provided data.  
- Working capital:  
  - 2024 working capital ~$679.4M, driven by large cash position and deferred revenue in current liabilities.  

Unit economics indicators  
- Subscription revenue growth (50% in 2024) significantly exceeded total revenue growth (41%), indicating positive mix shift to higher-value recurring revenue.  
- Gross margins remain ~72–73% despite increased AI costs and rising share of subscription revenue, suggesting favorable underlying unit economics in content delivery and infrastructure.  
- Paid subscriber penetration increased from ~8% to ~9% of MAUs between 2023 and 2024, with ARPU expansion in 2025 due to higher-tier adoption.  

TOPIC 4 – GROWTH OPPORTUNITIES  

TAM and secular trends  
- Language learning market:  
  - HolonIQ estimated ~2 billion people learning a language globally; consumer spend across online and offline language learning was ~a $60B market in earlier years, rising to ~$115B by 2025 and projected ~$123B by 2027.  
- Key secular trends the company highlights:  
  - Shift to mobile and app-based learning, with consumers expecting engaging, game-like experiences.  
  - Increasing global adoption of subscription models, including in education.  
  - Rapid advances in generative AI and large language models, which Duolingo expects will “change how people use technology to learn” and create significant opportunities for AI-native education platforms.  

Product and subject expansion  
- Beyond languages:  
  - Duolingo English Test: online, on-demand English proficiency test for high-stakes uses (university admissions, work visas, etc.), accepted by thousands of programs globally; leverages AI, computer-adaptive testing, and digital delivery.  
  - Duolingo ABC: early literacy app for young children launched in 2020, uses gamified methods; recognized with Time Magazine’s “Best New Product” award.  
  - Duolingo Math: launched 2022; focuses on elementary math and adult “brain training.”  
  - Duolingo Music: launched 2023 and integrated into the main app along with Math; as of the filing, Math and Music have not yet generated material revenue.  
- Platform leverage:  
  - All products (Duolingo App including language, Math, Music; Duolingo ABC; DET) share a “singular technology infrastructure,” allowing re-use of A/B testing, gamification, and personalization engines across new subjects.  

Monetization and ARPU expansion  
- Increasing paid penetration:  
  - Paid subscribers as a percentage of MAUs have been steadily rising (from ~8% in 2022 to ~9% in 2024).  
- Higher-value tiers:  
  - Duolingo Max introduces an AI-powered premium tier with more advanced features; expansion of Max is currently increasing AI costs but also lifts ARPU as users migrate to higher-priced plans.  
- Cross-product monetization:  
  - Potential to monetize Math, Music, and literacy once user bases are established; filings note the company often delays monetization of new products until engagement is at scale.  

Geographic and demographic expansion  
- Global spread:  
  - For some languages (e.g., Irish, Hawaiian), there are more learners on Duolingo than native speakers worldwide, indicating penetration of niche language communities.  
  - In the U.S., Duolingo’s language-learning user base exceeds the number of K–12 students taking a foreign language, implying runway among adult and nontraditional learners.  
- Socioeconomic breadth:  
  - Free access enables adoption among learners in lower-income regions and among refugees, potentially opening up new markets as smartphone penetration grows.  

Operational leverage and margin expansion  
- With revenue growth outpacing operating expense growth, Duolingo reports operating leverage across R&D, S&M, and G&A, on both GAAP and non‑GAAP bases.  
- Gross margin remains high despite incremental AI infrastructure costs, and FY 2025 guidance implies continued margin expansion at the Adjusted EBITDA level.  

M&A and strategic options (as implied by filings)  
- The company’s growth strategy is primarily organic and product-led, leveraging internal R&D and data/AI capabilities.  
- It has made at least one small acquisition (purchase of a business recorded in 2022 and 2024 cash flow statements) but no large-scale acquisitions appear in the provided data.  
- Filings emphasize internal innovation (new subjects, AI features, and tests) rather than M&A as the main driver of growth.  

This memo summarizes the available factual information on Duolingo’s competitive landscape, customer base, financial profile, and identified growth opportunities based on SEC filings and 2025 shareholder letters.
