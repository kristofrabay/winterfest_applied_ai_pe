Duolingo (DUOL) Research Memo  
Date: November 26, 2025  

TOPIC 1 – BUSINESS MODEL AND POSITIONING (CONTEXT)

• Business description:  
  – Duolingo, Inc. is an online education company headquartered in Pittsburgh, PA, focused on language learning and, increasingly, broader learning subjects. It offers mobile and web apps for language learning plus courses in math, music, and chess, and operates the Duolingo English Test (DET), an online English proficiency exam.([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duolingo?utm_source=openai))  

• Products and services:  
  – Core Duolingo app: gamified, bite-sized lessons with streaks, leaderboards, in-app currency and power-ups. Courses in 40+ languages.([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duolingo?utm_source=openai))  
  – Duolingo English Test: online, on-demand English proficiency test used for university admissions and other purposes; accepted by over 5,600 education programs globally, including 24 of the top 25 U.S. undergraduate programs by international enrollment (e.g., Yale, Stanford, MIT).  
  – New subjects: math (2022), music (2023), and chess (beta launched 2025) integrated into the main app.([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duolingo?utm_source=openai))  
  – Duolingo ABC: early literacy app for children (no ads or in-app purchases).([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duolingo?utm_source=openai))  

• Monetization:  
  – Freemium model: core learning content is free with ads. Paid subscription (“Super” and “Max”) removes ads and adds features (e.g., unlimited “energy”/hearts, generative-AI powered experiences).([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duolingo?utm_source=openai))  
  – Revenue streams (2023 breakdown): subscriptions, advertising, Duolingo English Test, in-app purchases, and other revenue (including a small physical restaurant concept). Subscriptions are the dominant revenue driver. In 2023, subscription revenue was $404.7m (48% YoY), advertising $49.9m (11% YoY), DET $41.2m (26% YoY), in‑app purchases $34.7m (94% YoY).  

• Scale and users:  
  – As of Q2 2025: 128.3m MAUs (+24% YoY), 47.7m DAUs (+40% YoY), and 10.9m paid subscribers (+37% YoY). Paid subscriber penetration ~9.0% of LTM MAUs.  
  – As of Dec 31, 2024: ~9% of MAUs were paid subscribers.  


TOPIC 2 – COMPETITION

2.1 Industry structure and competitive set

• Segment:  
  – Duolingo primarily operates in online, direct-to-consumer language learning and digital assessment, with adjacency to broader consumer edtech (math, music, chess). The industry is highly fragmented and characterized by low switching costs and frequent new entrants.  

• Stated risk factors on competition:  
  – Duolingo notes that the online language learning industry is “highly competitive, with low switching costs and a consistent stream of new products and entrants.” It competes not only with other online/app-based platforms but also with offline forms (classes, textbooks, tutoring). It also competes with language assessment providers and literacy platforms, and may compete with broader online learning platforms as it expands subjects.  
  – Many competitors have substantially greater financial and marketing resources, may offer more differentiated products (e.g., online learning plus physical classrooms and materials), or stronger positions in certain geographies/language pairs.  

• Key app-based language-learning competitors (consumer):  
  – Babbel – subscription language app geared toward adult conversational skills; offers 14 languages; no true free tier.([abblino.com](https://abblino.com/language-learning-apps-2025/?utm_source=openai))  
  – Rosetta Stone – long-standing immersion-based language platform; multi-platform; historically premium pricing.([abblino.com](https://abblino.com/language-learning-apps-2025/?utm_source=openai))  
  – Busuu – community-oriented language learning platform owned by Chegg, with >100m users.([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Busuu?utm_source=openai))  
  – Memrise – gamified vocabulary/spaced repetition, positioned similarly to Duolingo for casual learners.([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memrise?utm_source=openai))  
  – Mondly – multi-language app with VR/AR features, strong in VR ecosystems.([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mondly?utm_source=openai))  
  – Other apps frequently cited in comparisons: HelloTalk (social language exchange), LingoDeer, FluentU, Drops, Pimsleur app.([bestdevops.com](https://www.bestdevops.com/top-10-ai-language-learning-apps-tools-in-2025-features-pros-cons-comparison/?utm_source=openai))  

• High-stakes test competitors (Duolingo English Test):  
  – Competes with traditional English proficiency tests like TOEFL and IELTS, which are typically administered in physical test centers and priced higher; Duolingo’s filings emphasize DET as an online, lower-cost, more accessible alternative.  

• Emerging AI-native competitors:  
  – New entrants leveraging generative AI for conversational practice and tutoring, such as Speak (AI-powered English tutor backed by major investors and reportedly surpassing $100m annualized revenue; seen in media as a challenger to Duolingo).([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duolingo?utm_source=openai))  
  – Broader threat from large language models and conversational AI (e.g., ChatGPT, other AI assistants) providing free or low-cost language practice, which has been cited by analysts as an investor concern, though some see Duolingo as a beneficiary of AI rather than a victim.([marketwatch.com](https://www.marketwatch.com/story/the-ai-fears-that-hurt-this-stock-are-overblown-analyst-says-2b1c31e4?utm_source=openai))  

2.2 Market position and differentiation

• Scale and brand:  
  – Duolingo is one of the most-downloaded and widely recognized language-learning apps globally, with over 128m MAUs and 47.7m DAUs as of Q2 2025, and strong user engagement metrics (long streaks, high DAU/MAU ratio).  
  – Brand built around a distinctive mascot (Duo the owl) and viral social media marketing; widely recognized in consumer internet culture.([marketwatch.com](https://www.marketwatch.com/story/the-ai-fears-that-hurt-this-stock-are-overblown-analyst-says-2b1c31e4?utm_source=openai))  

• Product and technology:  
  – Heavy emphasis on gamification: streaks, leaderboards, rewards, “energy” system, cosmetic items, events; designed to make the app feel more like a mobile game than an education product.  
  – Data and AI: Duolingo positions its “learning flywheel” and “investment flywheel” as key advantages; larger user base generates more data to improve courses and personalization, which boosts effectiveness and engagement, driving more users and subscribers.  
  – Generative AI: subscription Max tier includes AI-powered features (e.g., role-play conversations, AI tutoring). Management has acknowledged gross margin pressure from AI costs but is emphasizing feature innovation and user engagement.  

• Competitive advantages cited by the company:  
  – Large, engaged global user base and data moat.  
  – Strong product-led growth model with relatively low paid marketing reliance.  
  – High gross margins (~72–73%) that support continued R&D investment.  

• Competitive disadvantages/pressures:  
  – Low switching costs; users can easily move between apps or use multiple tools.  
  – Competitors and AI-native startups may innovate with different pedagogical models (e.g., synchronous live tutors, VR immersion, voice-native experiences).([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mondly?utm_source=openai))  
  – Traditional tests (TOEFL/IELTS) remain entrenched with institutions, although DET has made significant inroads.  


TOPIC 3 – CUSTOMERS

3.1 Customer base and segments

• End users (learners):  
  – Primarily individual consumers worldwide who use Duolingo’s mobile and web apps. Courses cover a wide range of language pairs and increasingly math, music, chess; usage spans casual learners, students, travelers, professionals, and hobbyists.  
  – App is mobile-first and designed for short, daily sessions, appealing to casual and mobile-native users.  

• Institutional customers (DET):  
  – Universities and educational programs that accept the Duolingo English Test for admissions; >5,600 programs globally, including more than 4,700 higher-education programs.  
  – These institutions are not paying customers in the typical sense (students pay for tests), but institutional acceptance is critical to DET’s value proposition.  

• Geographic mix:  
  – Duolingo serves a global user base. Public disclosures highlight strong international usage and specific interest in China and other emerging markets, though recent 10-K excerpts in the provided files focus more on conceptual than geographic breakdowns.  

3.2 Customer behavior, engagement, and retention

• Engagement indicators:  
  – High DAU/MAU ratio: historically cited as a key engagement metric; earlier filings show DAU/MAU rising from ~24% to ~27% in prior years.  
  – Streaks: as of Dec 31, 2024 there were ~32m DAUs with a 7‑day streak or longer and ~10m DAUs with a 365‑day streak or longer, indicating long-term repeat usage among a subset of users.  

• Monetization and subscriber penetration:  
  – Company emphasizes that only a minority of users convert to paid plans, but penetration has risen: ~9% of MAUs as of Dec 31, 2024 and ~9.0% of LTM MAUs by Q2 2025 (10.9m subs vs. 128.3m MAUs).  
  – DET customers (test takers) are a separate paying segment, often linked to international study aspirations; revenue grew 26% YoY in 2023.  

• Customer concentration:  
  – Revenue is broadly diversified across millions of individual consumers; filings do not indicate material customer concentration in a small number of paying customers or institutions. (Standard SaaS-style “top customer” concentration disclosures are not prominent in available excerpts.)  

3.3 Pricing dynamics

• Subscription pricing:  
  – Duolingo offers monthly and annual subscription plans, including an annual family plan; pricing varies by geography and platform. The platform generally targets affordable consumer pricing compared to traditional courses, with in-app ads and IAPs augmenting ARPU.  
  – Q2 2025: subscription revenue per average paid subscriber (ARPU) grew 6% YoY, driven mainly by mix shift toward higher-priced tiers.  

• Test pricing:  
  – DET is marketed as significantly cheaper than traditional English tests (typically “hundreds of dollars” per test) and fully online, which is a key element in its competitive positioning.  

• Ad pricing:  
  – Advertising revenue growth has been slower than subscription growth; filings note advertising margins have at times been pressured by lower ad revenue per DAU.

TOPIC 5 – GROWTH OPPORTUNITIES

5.1 Expansion within core language learning

• User and subscriber growth runway:  
  – Given currently ~128m MAUs and 10.9m subscribers (~9% penetration), management believes there is “strong growth ahead” in subscriber base through product improvements, pricing optimization, and AI features.  
  – Large base of free users offers continued opportunity to expand both conversion to paid and ad/in-app purchase monetization.  

• Product improvements:  
  – Roadmap includes better content for intermediate and advanced learners, richer practice modes (e.g., matches in chess, more complex exercises in languages), and more social features.  
  – AI used to generate and scale new content (e.g., ~150 new language courses for different geographies generated via AI in Q1 2025) and to reduce per-unit content and tutoring costs.  

5.2 New subject verticals (beyond language)

• Math, music, and chess:  
  – Math and music courses launched in 2022–2023; chess launched in 2025 and reportedly grew faster than any prior subject launch, exceeding 1m DAUs by Q2 2025 on iOS in English only.  
  – Management believes similar gamified, mobile-first approach can extend to various academic subjects, further expanding addressable market.  

• Strategic rationale:  
  – New subjects leverage existing technology, gamification framework, and brand.  
  – Potential to attract new user segments (e.g., chess enthusiasts, parents, students) and deepen engagement among existing users, raising lifetime value and cross-sell into subscriptions.  

5.3 Duolingo English Test

• Adoption growth:  
  – DET adoption has increased steadily; >5,600 programs overall and >4,700 higher education programs accept it as proof of English proficiency, up materially from earlier years.  
  – The test benefits from structural trends: demand for international education, digitalization of assessments, and desire for lower-cost, more accessible testing options.  

• Monetization:  
  – DET revenue grew 26% YoY in 2023, and the test has high gross margins according to filings, benefiting from digital distribution and automated scoring.  

5.4 AI and personalization

• Internal use of AI:  
  – Generative AI is used to power features like Role Play, more human-like feedback, and to generate new exercises, enabling rapid course localization and content expansion at relatively lower marginal cost.  
  – Duolingo expects to continue optimizing models and infrastructure to reduce AI-related cost of revenue while expanding features, as evidenced by cost reductions achieved for Role Play.  

• Potential upside:  
  – Improved personalization could enhance outcomes and stickiness, raising conversion and retention.  
  – AI could also support new product lines (e.g., tutoring experiences) if cost-effective at scale.([reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/technology/duolingo-forecasts-annual-revenue-above-estimates-strong-adoption-ai-features-2025-02-27/?utm_source=openai))  

5.5 Geographic expansion and market penetration

• International growth opportunities:  
  – Management and media commentary emphasize strong opportunities in countries with growing middle class and smartphone penetration (e.g., emerging markets), where traditional education is expensive or less accessible.  
  – DET penetration into more countries’ institutions could further expand its TAM.  

5.6 M&A and partnerships

• To date:  
  – Limited evidence of large-scale M&A; filings show modest acquisitions (e.g., small business purchases in 2022 and 2024) but not transformative deals.  

• Potential directions (based on current model and industry structure):  
  – Acquisitions of niche education apps (e.g., specialized verticals, regional players) or AI education tools to accelerate subject expansion or localized content, given Duolingo’s large cash balance.  
  – Deeper partnerships with universities, language schools, or governments to integrate DET and potentially other offerings. (No major announced partnerships beyond DET acceptance in the provided documents.)  


SUMMARY

• Duolingo has scaled from ~$251m of revenue in 2021 to $748m in 2024 with sustained ~40–50% annual growth and expanding profitability (net income turning positive in 2023 and rising to $88.6m in 2024, with strong free cash flow).  
• The company operates within a highly competitive, low-switching-cost market with numerous app-based and traditional education competitors, plus emerging AI-native challengers. However, it benefits from a large and growing global user base, a strong gamified product, high gross margins, and a substantial cash position.  
• Growth opportunities include continued expansion of paid subscriber penetration, broader monetization of its large free user base, scaling of the Duolingo English Test, subject expansion (math, music, chess, and other high-demand topics), deeper AI-driven personalization, and geographic expansion.
