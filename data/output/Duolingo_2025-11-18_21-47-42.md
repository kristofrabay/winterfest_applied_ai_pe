Duolingo – Research Memo  
Date: November 19, 2025  

I. Company Overview and Business Model  

– Duolingo, Inc. is a U.S.-based mobile learning platform focused on language learning, with additional subjects in math, music, and chess. Founded in 2011, app launched in 2012; HQ in Pittsburgh, PA.   
– Public company (NASDAQ: DUOL); IPO completed July 30, 2021.   
– Core products and revenue streams:  
  • Freemium language app: all language content free, monetized via:  
    – Subscriptions (“Super Duolingo” / premium tiers, including AI-powered “Max”)  
    – In-app advertising to free users  
    – In-app purchases (virtual currency/power-ups like streak freezes, timer boosts)   
  • Duolingo English Test (DET): online English proficiency assessment, charged per test (historically ~$49); accepted by thousands of higher education programs globally.   
  • Newer non-language verticals: Duolingo Math and Music (launched 2023) and Chess (beta 2024–25). ([polygon.com](https://www.polygon.com/gaming/563109/duolingo-chess-lessons?utm_source=openai))  

– Scale (latest disclosed company/user data):  
  • Q2 FY25 (quarter ended June 30, 2025) shareholder letter:  
    – Daily Active Users (DAUs): 47.7M (up 40% YoY vs 34.1M in Q2 2024)  
    – Monthly Active Users (MAUs): 128.3M (up 24% YoY)  
    – Paid subscribers: 10.9M (up 37% YoY vs 8.0M)  
    – Paid subscriber penetration: 9.0% of LTM MAUs (vs 8.6% prior year)  
    – Q2 2025 revenue: $252.3M (+41% YoY)  
    – Q2 2025 net income: $44.8M (+84% YoY)  
    – Q2 2025 Adjusted EBITDA: $78.7M, 31.2% margin (vs 27.0% prior year).   

– Longer-term revenue trajectory:  
  • 2021 revenue: $250.8M; 2023 revenue: $531.1M; 2024 revenue (Yahoo Finance): $748.0M.   

– AI-first strategy:  
  • Duolingo is aggressively using generative AI for:  
    – Rapid course creation (148 new language courses launched in <1 year by reusing and adapting content via AI). ([theverge.com](https://www.theverge.com/news/658968/duolingo-language-courses-ai?utm_source=openai))  
    – Personalized practice (AI-led virtual conversation partners; personalization highlighted by CEO in 2025 Axios event). ([axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2025/04/04/axios-event-pittsburgh-local-ai-duolingo-education?utm_source=openai))  
  • Company states work that can be automated (e.g., some content creation) is being shifted from contractors to AI tools; AI proficiency is a hiring factor. ([theverge.com](https://www.theverge.com/news/658968/duolingo-language-courses-ai?utm_source=openai))  


II. Competition  

A. Market Position  

– Duolingo is widely cited as the leading global language-learning app:  
  • Business of Apps: Duolingo responsible for “about half” of all language-learning app revenues and ~60% of all language-learning app usage in 2023. ([businessofapps.com](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/language-learning-app-market/?utm_source=openai))  
  • Market/industry reports show Duolingo as top player with hundreds of millions of registered users and tens of millions of MAUs. ([marketgrowthreports.com](https://www.marketgrowthreports.com/market-reports/language-learning-app-market-102085?utm_source=openai))  

– Market share estimates (various sources, methodologies differ):  
  • Some market intel lists Duolingo with ~15–30% market share in language-learning platforms/software, ahead of Rosetta Stone and Babbel. ([strategicrevenueinsights.com](https://www.strategicrevenueinsights.com/industry/language-learning-platforms-market?utm_source=openai))  
  • Other data (usage-based) suggests ~50–60% of usage and roughly half of app revenue in the language learning app segment. ([businessofapps.com](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/language-learning-app-market/?utm_source=openai))  

B. Key Competitors  

– Direct language learning app competitors:  
  • Babbel – curriculum-based, conversation-focused; ~25M+ learners, strong in Europe; offers subscriptions and enterprise/education solutions. ([marketgrowthreports.com](https://www.marketgrowthreports.com/market-reports/language-learning-app-market-102085?utm_source=openai))  
  • Rosetta Stone – legacy brand, immersive method, strong institutional presence (schools, enterprises). ([datahorizzonresearch.com](https://datahorizzonresearch.com/language-learning-software-market-64655?utm_source=openai))  
  • Busuu – community features, social learning, peer feedback; tens of millions of users. ([marketgrowthreports.com](https://www.marketgrowthreports.com/market-reports/language-learning-app-market-102085?utm_source=openai))  
  • Memrise – vocabulary/mnemonics-centric, user-generated content. ([datahorizzonresearch.com](https://datahorizzonresearch.com/language-learning-software-market-64655?utm_source=openai))  
  • HelloTalk, Mondly, Lingoda, italki, Mango Languages, etc., with differentiated offerings (live tutoring, language exchange, VR, niche/professional courses). ([datahorizzonresearch.com](https://datahorizzonresearch.com/language-learning-software-market-64655?utm_source=openai))  

– Adjacent and indirect competitors:  
  • Massive Open Online Course/edtech platforms: Coursera, Udemy, etc. (language tracks). ([strategicrevenueinsights.com](https://www.strategicrevenueinsights.com/industry/language-learning-platforms-market?utm_source=openai))  
  • Offline/online blended providers and test-prep players (Berlitz, EF, Wall Street English, Pearson, local test-prep companies). ([datahorizzonresearch.com](https://datahorizzonresearch.com/language-learning-software-market-64655?utm_source=openai))  
  • Within new verticals (math, music, chess): competing with math apps (e.g., Photomath, Khan Academy Kids), music learning apps (e.g., Yousician, Simply Piano), and chess apps/sites (Chess.com, Lichess, etc. – not cited but relevant category).  

C. Competitive Advantages / Disadvantages (descriptive)  

Advantages (as indicated by data and industry commentary):  
– Scale and brand:  
  • Over 100M MAUs and 40M+ DAUs; widely recognized brand and mascot; featured as dominant usage/revenue share player. ([businessofapps.com](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/language-learning-app-market/?utm_source=openai))  
– Freemium, low friction distribution:  
  • Full core language content free; monetization built around optional upsell; large “top of funnel” and strong organic growth; selective paid marketing.   
– Data and experimentation:  
  • Hundreds of millions of exercises completed per day power extensive A/B testing and AI personalization; Duolingo emphasizes data-driven product optimization in filings.   
– Product gamification:  
  • Game-like UX and daily habit loops linked by third-party analysts and market reports to strong user engagement and retention. ([marketintelo.com](https://marketintelo.com/report/language-learning-apps-market?utm_source=openai))  
– AI capability and content velocity:  
  • Generative AI has allowed the company to more than double its course catalog (148 new courses) and pursue “AI tutor” features. ([theverge.com](https://www.theverge.com/news/658968/duolingo-language-courses-ai?utm_source=openai))  

Observed challenges / competitive pressures:  
– Highly competitive, low switching-cost market: multiple free or low-cost alternatives; users often multi-home across apps. ([datahorizzonresearch.com](https://datahorizzonresearch.com/language-learning-software-market-64655?utm_source=openai))  
– Need for continuous innovation to sustain engagement and conversion (noted across multiple industry analyses); niche competitors differentiate on depth or specialization (e.g., professional/technical language). ([finmodelslab.com](https://finmodelslab.com/products/specialized-language-learning-apps-five-forces?utm_source=openai))  
– For DET, multiple entrenched incumbents (TOEFL, IELTS, PTE) with long-standing institutional relationships.   


III. Customers  

A. User Base and Segmentation  

– Scale and growth:  
  • Q2 2025 MAUs: 128.3M; DAUs: 47.7M.   
  • MAU and DAU growth remains high double digits YoY.   

– Paid vs free users:  
  • Paid subscribers: 10.9M (Q2 2025); penetration ~9% of LTM MAUs.   
  • Historical penetration rates: 6.2% in 2021, 7.8% in 2022, 8.6% in 2024, showing gradual conversion improvement.   

– Customer segments (based on disclosures and market research):  
  1) Casual language learners (largest group)  
     – Global audience; mobile-first; short daily sessions; motivated by travel, culture, or hobby. ([marketintelo.com](https://marketintelo.com/report/language-learning-apps-market?utm_source=openai))  
  2) Students and young adults  
     – Use Duolingo as supplementary tool for school/university language courses; also DET takers for international admissions. Over 3,800 higher ed programs accepted DET as of 2022, including top U.S. universities.   
  3) Professionals seeking English or other skills for work  
     – Particularly in emerging markets (China, India, Latin America, Middle East) where English proficiency is valued; supported by regional market reports highlighting demand for English and other major languages. ([reportsnmarkets.com](https://www.reportsnmarkets.com/reports/global-language-learning-app-market-30765?utm_source=openai))  
  4) New subject learners (math, music, chess)  
     – Early-stage audiences; Duolingo has stated new subjects are selected where global demand and perceived societal benefit are high (e.g., math literacy, music education, chess). ([businessinsider.com](https://www.businessinsider.com/duolingo-ceo-criteria-new-subjects-added-chess-music-language-2025-5?utm_source=openai))  

– Geographic mix:  
  • Company and industry reports emphasize a global user base, with strong presence across North America, Europe, Asia (notably China), and Latin America.   

B. Customer Behavior and Retention  

– Engagement:  
  • DAU/MAU ratio (~37% based on 47.7M DAUs vs 128.3M MAUs) indicates relatively high engagement versus typical consumer apps.   
  • Gamified mechanics (streaks, leagues, badges, challenges) are core to daily return behavior; highlighted in filings and shareholder letters as growth drivers.   

– Retention / habit:  
  • Company stresses daily habit-building and long user tenures; many subscribers reportedly use the app for months or years before upgrading.   
  • Churn metrics are not fully broken out publicly, but management commentary suggests improving subscriber retention alongside product improvements. (Qualitative, drawn from earnings/shareholder communications.)   

– Customer concentration:  
  • Revenue is diversified across millions of paying subscribers and hundreds of millions of free users.  
  • No indication of dependency on a small number of enterprise or institutional customers; DET is sold to individuals, not contracted bulk buyers, though institutional acceptance influences demand.   

C. Pricing and Monetization  

– Subscription pricing:  
  • Historically, subscription plans (monthly/annual) priced in the range of roughly ~$7–$13/month equivalent, varying by geography and promotions; SEC filings emphasize multi-length time-based subscriptions up to 12 months.   
  • Premium offerings include ad-free learning, unlimited hearts/lives, additional practice modes, and higher-tier AI features (Duolingo Max) with conversational practice and explanations generated by LLMs. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duolingo?utm_source=openai))  

– Advertising:  
  • Monetization of free users via display and video ads served through major programmatic networks; revenue scales with free-user base and engagement hours.   

– In-app purchases (IAPs):  
  • Users can buy virtual currency (Gems) and power-ups to enhance gamification (streak freezes, timer boosts, extra hearts).   

– Duolingo English Test pricing:  
  • Filings cite a one-time test fee (historically $49) as a one-off purchase; DET monetization depends on global university acceptance and test-taker volume.

V. Growth Opportunities  

A. Market Expansion  

– Overall language-learning market:  
  • Global language learning software/apps market expected to continue growing through 2030+, driven by globalization, remote work/study, and demand for English and other major languages. Multiple reports project continued double-digit CAGR for language learning software/apps. ([datahorizzonresearch.com](https://datahorizzonresearch.com/language-learning-software-market-64655?utm_source=openai))  
  • Duolingo is cited as having “over 500M registered users” and >70M MAUs as of 2024, suggesting substantial untapped conversion potential even within its current base. ([marketgrowthreports.com](https://www.marketgrowthreports.com/market-reports/language-learning-app-market-102085?utm_source=openai))  

– Geographic expansion:  
  • Duolingo targets growth in Asia (notably China) and Latin America, where demand for English and other languages is rising; company has operations in the U.S., China, Germany and historically emphasized emerging markets in filings.   

B. Product & Subject Expansion  

– New subjects:  
  • Math and Music (launched 2023) and Chess (2024–25 rollout) represent moves beyond language into broader education. Early data point: chess course reportedly had >1M DAUs on iOS/English alone by Q2 2025.   
  • CEO has articulated criteria for new subjects: large global demand, societal/educational benefit, internal enthusiasm. Coding and Pokémon were explicitly rejected for not meeting these criteria (limited global demand, insufficient educational value). ([businessinsider.com](https://www.businessinsider.com/duolingo-ceo-criteria-new-subjects-added-chess-music-language-2025-5?utm_source=openai))  

– AI-driven personalization and tutoring:  
  • AI is being used to create “virtual tutors” and conversation practice; AI-first approach may enable expansions into more complex subjects and more individualized learning paths. ([theverge.com](https://www.theverge.com/news/658968/duolingo-language-courses-ai?utm_source=openai))  

– Content breadth and depth:  
  • AI-enabled course generation allowed 148 new language courses across 28 UI languages in under a year, substantially expanding long-tail course offerings and localization. ([theverge.com](https://www.theverge.com/news/658968/duolingo-language-courses-ai?utm_source=openai))  

C. Monetization and ARPU Upside  

– Subscription penetration:  
  • Paid subscribers represent ~9% of MAUs; prior years show a rising trend from ~6–7%. Increasing penetration (e.g., via better paywall experiences, new premium features, family/enterprise plans) could drive ARPU growth.   

– Layered premium tiers:  
  • Introduction of AI-rich “Max” tier likely raises ARPU among a subset of users; potential to expand advanced tiers (e.g., professional, exam-prep, certificate bundles). ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duolingo?utm_source=openai))  

– Duolingo English Test and certification:  
  • DET is accepted by thousands of institutions and priced below legacy exams; increased adoption across universities and potentially employers/governments offers expansion opportunity.   

– Non-language subjects monetization:  
  • Math, Music, Chess and any future subjects can be monetized either as part of existing subscription tiers or as specialized offerings; strategy is still emerging. ([polygon.com](https://www.polygon.com/gaming/563109/duolingo-chess-lessons?utm_source=openai))  

D. M&A and Strategic Partnerships  

– While no major recent acquisitions are highlighted in the materials provided, filings show small bolt-on acquisitions (e.g., business purchase entries in cash flow statement).

([theverge.com](https://www.theverge.com/news/658968/duolingo-language-courses-ai?utm_source=openai))

VI. Summary of Key Factual Points  

– Duolingo is the leading global language-learning app by usage and revenue share, with >100M MAUs and >40M DAUs. ([businessofapps.com](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/language-learning-app-market/?utm_source=openai))  
– Business model is freemium, with primary monetization from subscriptions, supplemented by ads, IAP, and the Duolingo English Test.   
– Financially, Duolingo has grown revenue from $250.8M (2021) to $748.0M (2024), moved from net losses to ~$88.6M net income in 2024, and generated ~$264M of free cash flow in 2024 with substantial net cash on the balance sheet.   
– Competitive landscape includes Babbel, Rosetta Stone, Busuu, Memrise, HelloTalk, and others, across both consumer and institutional channels. Duolingo’s gamified and AI-driven approach is repeatedly cited as a differentiator. ([datahorizzonresearch.com](https://datahorizzonresearch.com/language-learning-software-market-64655?utm_source=openai))  
– Growth opportunities include deeper penetration of existing user base, geographical expansion (especially in emerging markets), subject expansion (math, music, chess, future topics), further AI-driven personalization and cost efficiencies, and broader adoption of DET and other credentialing products.
